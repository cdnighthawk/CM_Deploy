Safety Management – Detailed Breakdown
This is a new but critical module specifically added to address your request for both company-wide and site-specific safety. It integrates deeply with Daily Logs, DrawingViewer, Local AI, Punch Lists, RFIs, and Financials.
Front-end: W3CRM (Plan\0). Prefer construction\*.html demos as visual references; implement as usis-safety-*.html + Bootstrap forms.
1. Core Philosophy

Dual Layer Approach: Company-wide standards + Project-specific execution.
AI-Powered: Local Llama 4 Scout reviews field photos for hazards, PPE compliance, and unsafe conditions.
Proactive & Compliant: Designed for California government/commercial requirements (OSHA, Cal/OSHA, Title 24 safety elements).
Tightly Integrated: Safety findings automatically create Punch List items, RFIs, or Change Orders.

2. Two Main Sub-Modules
A. Company-Wide Safety (Global / Admin Level)

Centralized safety policies and manual (stored in Backblaze B2)
Standard safety checklists, Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) templates, and Emergency Response Plans
Employee training records & certifications (OSHA 10/30, First Aid, Scaffold, Fall Protection, etc.)
Company-wide incident history, lessons learned database, and safety KPI tracking (TRIR, DART, Near-Miss Rate)
Safety bulletin board / company alerts
Safety dashboard for leadership (trends, compliance score)

B. Site / Project-Specific Safety

Project-specific safety plan & site orientation documents
**Project JHAs** — living Job Hazard Analysis documents **for the job** (see §9); distinct from company JHA **templates** in §2A.
**Daily pretask** (same workflow as **project pretask**) — crew completes **each workday** before production work; references the applicable project JHA (see §10); integrated with Daily Logs / field ops (Plan 8).
Daily Safety Logs (integrated into Daily Reports)
Crew safety checklist
Hazard identification & controls (JHA per task / activity row on a project JHA)
PPE verification per trade
Site condition / weather assessment

Safety Observations & Near-Miss reporting (quick mobile form + photo)
Incident / Injury reporting (with mandatory fields for government compliance)
Corrective Action tracking (linked to Punch List)
Subcontractor safety compliance (tied to RFP awards)

9. Project Job Hazard Analysis (project JHAs)
---------------------------------------------
Purpose: Maintain **formal, versioned JHAs on the project** so crews and subs align on hazards and controls for that site—not only generic company templates.

• **Scope:** One project may have **multiple** project JHAs (e.g. by building, phase, major trade package, or high-risk activity cluster). Each `project_jhas` row has a version number; edits create a new version or explicit revision per company policy; submitted pretasks should reference **revision** for audit if the JHA changes mid-job.

• **Content model:** Structured steps (`project_jha_steps`): activity/task, identified hazards, engineering/administrative/PPE controls, responsible party, date reviewed. Attachments (photos, SDS refs) via Documents hub `document_id` links.

• **Templates:** Users may “Create from template” pulling from `jha_templates` / `usis-jha-library.html`, then edit for site-specific conditions.

• **Approvals (optional phase):** Major JHA revisions may enter the shared approval engine (Plan 14) before marked `active` for field use.

• **API/UI:** CRUD under `/api/v1/projects/<id>/project-jhas` (shape TBD); list + detail pages as in §3.

10. Daily pretask (project pretask)
------------------------------------
Same practice, two labels used in the field: **daily pretask** and **project pretask**. In this platform both mean: **the per-day, per-crew (or per-shift) pre-job safety brief and hazard review** completed **with field ops as part of the daily rhythm**, before production quantities are logged.

• **When:** **Each calendar workday** (or each shift if you later split shifts) that the crew works on the project; configurable rule: “cannot submit daily production log without a submitted pretask for that date/crew.”

• **Who:** Foreman / competent person (logged `User`); optional worker acknowledgment list (names or headcount + “all present reviewed”).

• **What:** (1) Confirm **today’s work scope** aligns with schedule/task or free-text scope line. (2) **Review** linked **project JHA** section(s) or attach “reviewed JHA v3” acknowledgment—store `project_jha_id` + `jha_version` snapshot on `daily_pretasks`. (3) Site/weather, nearby utilities, hot work, fall hazard reminders as checkboxes. (4) **PPE** verification. (5) Emergency muster / nearest hospital (optional fields). (6) **Signatures** (foreman + optional crew attestation).

• **Where in UI:** Primary path: **embedded** in Plan 8 daily log / `usis-field.html` as first tab or accordion “Daily pretask” using `elements/usis-daily-pretask-form.html`. Secondary: standalone `usis-daily-pretask.html` with `?project_id=&date=` for phone-first crews.

• **Offline:** Queue pretask JSON same as other field forms (Plan 13); server rejects duplicate (project_id, work_date, crew_lead_user_id) unless policy allows amend flow.

• **Reporting:** Filter pretasks by project/date; export for GC or Cal/OSHA inspection packages alongside `daily_safety_logs`.

This module turns safety from a compliance burden into a proactive, AI-assisted competitive advantage — reducing incidents, speeding up corrective actions, and protecting margins on California government projects.
