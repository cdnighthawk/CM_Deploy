Sage CM — Subcontracts, SCO, Procure-to-Pay (extension to Plan PO review)
============================================================================

Purpose
-------
Confirm and document how Sage Construction Management extends beyond purchase
orders (POs) into subcontracts and subcontract change orders (SCO), and where
Intacct-style procure-to-pay (P2P) controls sit. This complements the PO-focused
summary in the Sage CM PO review plan and ties behaviors to USIS_CM schema
direction (commitments, bill allocations, workflow locks).

1. Subcontracts vs POs (Sage product behavior)
----------------------------------------------
From Sage CM procurement help (same family as the PO overview):

- POs: no retainage in the PO path; vendor bills tie via PO-to-Bill.
- Subcontracts: retainage / holdbacks and scope changes are handled on a
  subcontract track, not the same as a simple PO line lifecycle.

Use Sage help for authoritative wording:
- Purchase orders overview:
  https://help.sagecm.intacct.com/Content/Modules/Procurement/POs/POsOverview.htm
- Subcontract change orders (SCO) overview:
  https://help.sagecm.intacct.com/Content/Modules/Procurement/SCO/SCOOverview.htm

2. Subcontract change orders (SCO)
----------------------------------
Per SCO overview documentation:

- SCOs modify an existing subcontract when scope or commercial terms change;
  either party may participate depending on configuration.
- Status progression parallels PO-style governance: Draft → Pending Submission →
  Pending → Not Approved → Approved (exact labels per Sage UI/help).
- An SCO affects budget / committed views when Approved with a status date
  (similar “effective approval date” idea to PO → committed cost).
- Preconditions echo POs: prime contract and base subcontract approved with
  status dates; job cost codes on the project; optional tax codes.
- Creation paths include manual entry and flows from CPR / CO / WO (same
  procurement wizard family as POs).
- Line items support cost codes, quantities, unit pricing, and imports from
  labor/equipment/work databases where licensed.

USIS_CM mapping (directional): model SCO as either (a) a child entity under a
subcontract commitment with its own approval/status dates, or (b) a specialized
change-order record linked to a subcontract commitment. Phase 1 in code uses a
single `commitments` table with `commitment_kind = subcontract` and optional
`retention_percentage`; SCO can be a follow-on migration adding
`subcontract_change_orders` when financials UI ships.

3. Procure-to-pay (P2P) and workflow controls
---------------------------------------------
PO overview already states: if a workflow rule for the PO is initiated or
approved, the PO cannot be edited unless the rule is abandoned.

Broader Intacct/Sage documentation describes organization-level P2P controls
(segregation of duties, approval chains, matching). For construction-specific
PO behavior, stay on Sage CM procurement help; for enterprise control concepts,
see Intacct procure-to-pay / workflow control topics (separate help tree from
pure “construction” PO screens).

USIS_CM mapping: `commitments.workflow_rule_active` (boolean) mirrors the
“locked while workflow in flight” flag at header level; future refinements can
add a workflow instance table, approver steps, and abandonment events.

4. Bill linkage (PO → many bills)
---------------------------------
Same as PO overview: one PO may drive multiple vendor bills; PO-to-Bill wizard
is used repeatedly. USIS_CM stores each slice in `commitment_bill_allocations`
with amount and vendor bill reference until a first-class `invoices` table exists.
